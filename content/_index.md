## What is PedalKernel?

PedalKernel is an **open source Wave Digital Filter (WDF) framework** written in Rust for modeling analog guitar effects circuits.

We simulate the actual circuit — resistors, capacitors, op-amps, transistors — at the component level. The math preserves the physical relationships between elements, which means dynamic response, interactive controls, and non-linear behavior that happens naturally.

## Open Source (Open Core)

**The kernel is free and open source** under AGPLv3. Build whatever you want. Fork it. Hack on it. Just share your improvements.

**Commercial licensing is available** for proprietary products. Our partner **[Puget Audio](https://puget.audio/)** sells professional VST3 plugins built on PedalKernel.

## PedalKernel Pro

**PedalKernel Pro** is how we work with companies building commercial products on our technology — hardware manufacturers, plugin developers, and audio companies.

We offer licensing for:
- White-label VST/CLAP/AU/AAX plugins
- DSP implementations for hardware
- Custom circuit modeling and development

Our consumer plugins are distributed by **[Puget Audio](https://puget.audio/)** as **Puget Legends** — a collection of meticulously modeled classic pedals and studio processors.

## FAQ

**Q: Is PedalKernel really free?**  
A: Yes. AGPLv3 licensed.

**Q: Can I use this commercially?**  
A: The open source kernel is AGPLv3. For proprietary use, see [PedalKernel Pro](/pro/) for licensing options.

**Q: Do I need to know Rust?**  
A: To hack on the kernel, yes. To use the plugin, no.

**Q: What's the difference between the kernel and Pro?**  
A: The kernel is the open source engine. Pro is our commercial licensing program for companies building products on that engine.
