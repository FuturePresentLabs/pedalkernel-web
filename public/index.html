What is PedalKernel?

PedalKernel is an open source Wave Digital Filter (WDF) framework written in Rust for modeling analog guitar effects circuits.

We simulate the actual circuit — resistors, capacitors, op-amps, transistors — at the component level. The math preserves the physical relationships between elements, which means dynamic response, interactive controls, and non-linear behavior that happens naturally.

Open Source (Open Core)

The kernel is free and open source under AGPLv3. Build whatever you want. Fork it. Hack on it. Just share your improvements.

Commercial licensing is available for proprietary products. Our partner Puget Audio sells professional VST3 plugins built on PedalKernel.

FAQ

Q: Is PedalKernel really free?
A: Yes. AGPLv3 licensed.

Q: Can I use this commercially?
A: The open source kernel is AGPLv3. For proprietary use, contact us about licensing.

Q: Do I need to know Rust?
A: To hack on the kernel, yes. To use the plugin, no.

Q: What’s the difference between the kernel and Pro?
A: The kernel is the engine. Pro is a car built on that engine — polished UI, more effects, professional support.
